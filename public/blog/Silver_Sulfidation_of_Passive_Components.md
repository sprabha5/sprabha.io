I recenetly got to learn about the Silver sulfidation of passive components.
The failure of zneer diode on a motor drive board OCV circuit due external package-level failure caused by sulfur-induced corrosion but not becuase of internal Silicon die.
Mechanism: Sulfur ($S$) reacts with the silver ($Ag$) plating or internal lead interface of the DO-35 glass package. This creates a conductive silver sulfide ($Ag_{2}S$) film that creeps along the non-hermetic glass-to-lead interface.

From this i got to know the significance of components package significance to avoice this type of issue and mitigate and failure prevention.
There are different ways in which the analysis to detect silver sulfidation like SEM/EDS (Scanning Electron Microscopy - Energy Dispersive X-ray Spectrometry) analysis.
The sulfur creates a "bridge" or leakage path outside the die of the components which creates short circuit, and failure of the circuit in a application.
The silver sulfidation is hard to detect form outside, the presence of it can be identified by various techniques, SEM/EDS where sulfur can be seen on leads, package.

The silver sulfidation of zener diode which is used in  a circuit to clamp or for portection where these diodes fail "short" due to the external sulfur path, they shunt the signal to ground or the return rail prematurely.
